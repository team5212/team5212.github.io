About

Entering our fifth year as competitors in the FIRST Robotics competition, TAMSformers prides itself on its two year fast track leadership program. Our two year fast track leadership program allows us to prepare members of our team for successful scholarship and careers in the future as demonstrated by the accomplishments of our alumni. The program is based on our FIRST competition inspired philosophy which is comprised of five different points: Activities, Mentors, Integrity, Learning, and Youth. Our program, through FIRST, allows us to participate in various ACTIVITIES, both within our community and throughout others, that allow us develop ourselves as a team and also to inspire a passion for engineering in others. We rely on MENTORS to help guide and advise members of our program through various problems we are not able to navigate by ourselves. Our program emphasizes INTEGRITY is one of the most important aspects of our team; we strive to demonstrate it both on our team and outside of it. Another important aspect of our program is its encouragement of LEARNING; not only are we striving to learn more about engineering and robotics, but trying to improve and encourage learning within the community. Each year we spend thousands of hours within the community--through activities like our free STEM camps and experiment boxes, to name a few--hoping to inspire the YOUTH within our community to pursue STEM fields.

Outreach

  • • We host a yearly summer camp which gives 60+ kids from low income families to have a STEM experience and encourage their growth in such directions.
  • • We perform Science demonstrations at local schools in the Denton Area through our Sci Demos Program.
  • • We support two local Girls in Engineering programs at local schools and libraries and are working towards expanding our footprint in this area.
  • • We annually donate 75+ Stem boxes to local Denton Libraries in order to increase our STEM footprint in the community. Each box contains simple experiments that can be performed at home with regular household ingredients.
  • • We received a proclamation from the Mayor of Denton for the Stem boxes.
  • • We mentor 2 FLL teams in the area and hope to expand our mentoring program in the future.
  • • We tutor an elementary student at our campus.



Hurricane Harvey

As you all may know, Hurricane Harvey has negatively impacted many communities in South and Central Texas. This includes many of our fellow FRC teams and their families and communities. Through the Harvey Fund, the FRC teams who have been affected by Hurricane Harvey will still be able to afford to compete. With the money raised, we will be able to help lift the additional financial burden of competing in FRC. Please help us raise money for these teams who may no longer be able to compete due to such disastrous and financial circumstances. We hope everybody is safe and we look forward to seeing our FIRST family at competition this season. Click here to donate.
